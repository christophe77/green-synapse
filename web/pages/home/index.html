Cannabis Breeding Plan

Cannabis Breeding Plan for Award-Winning Autoflower Strain

Objective

To create an award-winning autoflower strain with red flowers and a strawberry flavor.

Recommended Strains for Crossing

  • Purpleberry Autoflower - Known for its purple/red hues and berry flavors.
  • Strawberry Banana Autoflower - Offers a strong strawberry flavor profile.
  • Red Poison Autoflower - Known for its red and purple colors and fruity aroma.

Breeding Plan

Phase 1: Initial Cross

Cross Purpleberry Autoflower with Strawberry Banana Autoflower to combine the red/purple hues with the strawberry flavor.

  • Select a male Purpleberry Autoflower plant with the most intense red/purple colors.
  • Select a female Strawberry Banana Autoflower plant with the strongest strawberry flavor.
  • Pollinate the female plant with the male pollen.
  • Collect the seeds from the female plant.

Phase 2: Backcrossing

Backcross the F1 generation with Red Poison Autoflower to enhance the red colors and fruity aroma.

  • Grow the F1 seeds and select the best phenotype with red flowers and strawberry flavor.
  • Cross this selected F1 female with a male Red Poison Autoflower plant.
  • Collect the seeds from the female plant.

Phase 3: Stabilization

Stabilize the desired traits through selective breeding over several generations.

  • Grow the backcrossed seeds and select the best phenotypes with red flowers and strawberry flavor.
  • Self-pollinate the selected females or cross them with selected males from the same generation.
  • Repeat this process for at least 3-4 generations to stabilize the traits.

Additional Tips

  • Maintain detailed records of each cross and the characteristics of the offspring.
  • Use controlled environments to minimize external factors affecting the phenotypes.
  • Consider using marker-assisted selection (MAS) for precise trait selection.

Recommended Products

  • Purpleberry Autoflower
  • Strawberry Banana Autoflower
  • Red Poison Autoflower

Conclusion

By following this breeding plan, you can create a unique autoflower strain with red flowers and a strawberry flavor that has the potential to win awards at the Cannabis Cup. Good luck with your breeding project!

Cannabis Breeding & Competition Preparation

1. Detailed Variety Sheets

Blackout (Indica with Black Leaves)

Objectives:

  • Unique appearance: Black leaves and flowers.
  • Powerful effect: "Blackout" effect with high THC (>25%).
  • Earthy, coffee, or peppery taste.
  • Indica-dominant genetics (>80%).

Initial Cross: Hokkaido Hemp x Black Domina (pure Indica with dark leaves).

Genetic Stabilization:

  • Selective crossings in F2, F3 to enhance the black appearance and intense effect.
  • Cloning for genetic consistency.
  • Regular THC, CBD, and terpene tests.

Target Competitions:

  • Emerald Cup (Month 22) — First presentation in California to showcase the potent effects.
  • High Times Cannabis Cup (Year 3) — Stabilized variety presentation.

Deep Purple (Improved Purple Haze)

Objectives:

  • Retain the classic fruity Purple Haze flavor (grapes, berries, floral).
  • Increase potency and/or prolong the effects.
  • Sativa-dominant genetics (>70%).

Initial Cross: Purple Haze x Amnesia Haze (Sativa with prolonged and energetic effects).

Genetic Stabilization:

  • Selective crossings in F2, F3 to enhance effect duration.
  • Cloning for consistency in flavor and effects.
  • Regular tests for THC (>22%) and lasting effect.

Target Competitions:

  • Spannabis Champions Cup (Month 24) — Showcasing the enhanced Sativa effects.
  • Highlife Cup (Year 3) — Final test of the stabilized variety.

Yellow Submarine (Yellow Flowers, Banana Flavor)

Objectives:

  • First Indica with yellow flowers.
  • Distinct banana flavor, sweet and creamy.
  • Relaxing body effect with potent relaxation (Indica-dominant >80%).

Initial Cross: Hokkaido Hemp x Banana Kush (fruit-flavored Indica).

Genetic Stabilization:

  • Selecting phenotypes with prominent yellow pistils.
  • Intensive cloning to secure yellow flower and banana flavor traits.
  • Flavor, potency tests (THC >23%), and terpenes (banana, fruity).

Target Competitions:

  • Emerald Cup (Month 22) — Presentation of the first yellow Indica.
  • High Times Cannabis Cup (Year 3) — Final stabilized variety presentation.

Big 4 (Hybrid of the 4 Most Famous Strains)

Objectives:

  • Blend the legendary traits of OG Kush, Sour Diesel, White Widow, and Blue Dream.
  • Balanced effects: Body relaxation with mild mental stimulation.
  • Complex flavor: Mix of woody, diesel, fruity, and sweet notes.
  • High resin production.

Initial Cross:

  • OG Kush x Sour Diesel (Hybrid 1 - Energizing and relaxing effects, woody and diesel flavor).
  • White Widow x Blue Dream (Hybrid 2 - Balanced effect, sweet and floral flavor).
  • Cross of both hybrids to obtain Big 4.

Genetic Stabilization:

  • Intensive cloning to ensure balanced traits.
  • Regular tests to adjust terpene profile and overall effect.
  • Genetic marker-assisted selection (if available).

Target Competitions:

  • Spannabis Champions Cup (Month 24) — First presentation with distinct traits.
  • Highlife Cup (Year 3) — Final competition for assessing overall stability.

2. Competition Submission: Presentation Dossier

Each competition submission should include the following sections:

  1. Variety Name & General Description
  2. Genetic Lineage Details: Parents and successive crosses.
  3. Selection Objectives: Effects, appearance, and flavor goals.
  4. Cannabinoid Profile: THC, CBD, CBG levels (if relevant).
  5. Terpene Profile: Dominant terpenes.
  6. Photos: Of plants, clones, and dried flowers.
  7. Laboratory Test Information: If available.
  8. Attractive Visual Presentation: Packaging and logos, if needed.

3. Laboratory Protocol (Detailed Step-by-Step)

Phase 1: Initial Cross & Cultivation (Months 1-4)

  1. Germination: Grow each selected variety separately.
  2. Controlled Environment: 24°C, 70% humidity, 18 hours/day light cycle.
  3. Manual Pollination: Controlled pollination using plastic bags to ensure precise crossbreeding.
  4. F1 Seed Production: Grow F1 seeds for further selection.

Phase 2: Preliminary Selection & Cloning (Months 5-8)

  1. F1 Germination: Germinate a large batch (~100 seeds per variety) to maximize the discovery of desirable phenotypes.
  2. Early Growth in Hydroponics: Fast-growing setups to observe early traits.
  3. Phenotype Selection: Assess vigor, appearance, odor, and growth characteristics. Early cannabinoid and terpene testing.
  4. Cloning: Take clones from selected plants (3 clones per plant). Maintain clones in a cloning chamber (24°C, 80% humidity, low light 18h/day).

Phase 3: Genetic Stabilization (Months 9-18)

  1. Selective Crossings (F2): Cross selected clones to enhance desired traits.
  2. F3 Generation Stabilization: Continue crossings to stabilize desired characteristics. Clone plants for consistency.
  3. Regular Testing: Monitor cannabinoid levels and terpene profiles.

Phase 4: Final Testing & Cloning (Months 19-24)

  1. Testing in Real Conditions: Grow the plants both indoors and outdoors to assess their stability in various environments.
  2. Mass Cloning: Clone the best-performing plants to secure genetic consistency.
  3. Final Analysis: Confirm desired traits and ensure stability before competition submission.

Phase 5: Competition Preparation (Months 21-36)

  1. Clone Selection: Choose the best clones for competition submission.
  2. Sample Preparation: Drying, curing, and vacuum packing the samples.
  3. Competition Submission: Submit your varieties to competitions like High Times Cannabis Cup, Emerald Cup, Spannabis, etc.
